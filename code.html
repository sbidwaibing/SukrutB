    A Survey: Extraction of River Networks from Satellite Images

    Abstract:
    Extracting river networks is essential for monitoring water resources. Various methods, including image processing, predefined models, and custom algorithms, have been developed and combined over time. This survey examines these techniques and their outcomes, focusing on satellite image-based methods. Image processing often employs clustering and threshold segmentation, while predefined models like CNN, ResUNet, YOLO, Faster CNN, and MSCFF are also used. However, these may not always provide high accuracy. Thus, this paper emphasizes approaches for extracting river networks specifically from satellite images.

    Apr 04, 2023

    Extraction of River Networks from Satellite Images using Image Processing & Deep Learning Techniques

    Abstract:
    River networks are analyzed for various purposes, including locating water bodies, examining river gauge levels, predicting flows, and conserving energy amid global warming. Their extraction from digital imagery involves segmentation and machine learning. This study utilizes datasets from Kaggle and Google Earth Engine, employing methods like image segmentation, gray scaling, enhancement, global thresholding, and the UNet deep learning architecture. The developed UNet model achieves an 80.98% dice score in extracting river networks from satellite images, showing promise for application in various semantic segmentation detection datasets.
